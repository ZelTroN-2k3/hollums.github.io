My Dad started teaching me guitar when I was 14. Mason Williams' "Classical Gas", classic Rock and Folk songs, jazzy arrangements by Charlie Byrd, this is where I started. From there, I started following some of the CandyRat artists on YouTube. My favorites were Antoine Dufour and Andy McKee. They really opened my eyes to the unique sounds that can only be found in open tunings. Their technique of integrating fretboard tapping and harmonics really interested me to learn that eccentric style they've trademarked.

In high school, my music partner in crime, Karla Dietmeyer, taught me a lot as well. Together, we arranged a CD called "The Departure" under the name "TenString Serenade." Later on while I was in college, I continued to dabble on the guitar, but eventually picked up with playing the drums for fun with my room mates. I learned how to play in a larger group and improvise, and just enjoy the "jam" as we'd call it. By the end of my college career, I've developed a rudimentary understanding of the bass, guitar, drums, harmonica and even just a little bit of singing.

Eventually, I would like to know how to play as many instruments as possible - enough to "dabble" the way I do with my room mates. Each instrument has a offers a different way of enjoying music, so the more, the merrier. For now, I am picking back up the guitar and working on writing music for the games I plan to develop in the future.
